# Notes on the GSCI Live Cattle Methodology

## Livestock Production Quantities 

The annual production quantity for cattle—which
is stated in terms of carcass weight—is converted into an equivalent quantity of
live cattle by multiplying the production quantity of cattle for a given year by
the ratio of the live weight of cattle to the dressed weight of cattle (ALW/ADW)
for that year. In addition, cattle and hog production quantities are based on world
industrial production data, rather than total world production data.

# Claude Summary

## Contract Details

Live cattle is traded on the CME exchange under ticker LC (RIC code). The
contract size is 40,000 lbs, with a conversion factor of 2,204.62 between
contract units (lbs) and World Production Quantity units (1,000 metric tonnes).

## Weighting & Production Data 

Cattle production weights are based on world industrial production data rather
than total world production. Annual carcass weight figures are converted to live
weight equivalents by multiplying by the ratio of live weight to dressed weight
(ALW/ADW) for each year. The 2023 World Production Average was 64,585,598
thousand metric tonnes, up 1.6% from the 2022 figure of 63,586,878. The 2023
Contract Production Weight (CPW) was 110,633.0, marginally down from 110,644.1
in 2022. The 2023 Reference Percentage Dollar Weight was 2.98%, down from 3.76%
in 2022, reflecting the lower average contract reference price of $1.363 per lb
used in the 2022 calculation period.

## Contract Roll Schedule

Live cattle uses the following designated contract expiration months (starting
January): G, J, J, M, M, Q, Q, V, V, Z, Z, G — meaning the index holds February,
April, June, August, October and December contracts across the calendar year.
Enhanced Index Variation

In the S&P GSCI Enhanced Commodity Index, live cattle follows a different roll
schedule, rotating semi-annually between only the April (J) and October (V)
contracts — rolling into April during the September roll period and into October
during the March roll period. Multiple Contract Index

In the Multiple Contract Index, live cattle is held across three contract
"clones" (LC-A, LC-B, LC-C) with staggered expiration schedules to distribute
exposure across the futures curve, weighted 55%/30%/15% across the base, 1-month
forward, and 2-month forward contracts respectively.
